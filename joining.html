Joining

Interested in becoming a member of the Cornell Grilling Club? We welcome grill enthusiasts of all levels and backgrounds to join us! Membership requirements include attending a handful of events per semester and completing the application process. As a member, you can attend our events, workshops, and activities, as well as the opportunity to connect with other grill enthusiasts on campus.

How to Join/ Membership Requirements

Club Expectations

  • Members are expected to attend as many events as they can and to communicate with the leadership if they are unable to.
  • Members are expected to actively participate in club activities, including volunteering for events and helping to plan events.
  • Members are expected to treat their fellow members with respect and courtesy. They also are expected to create a safe and welcoming environment for all other memeber
  • Members are expected to take responsibility for their own actions and to uphold the values of the club at all times.
  • Members are expected to be open-minded towards learning from others and to share their own knowledge with the community.
  • Members are encouraged to develop their leadership skills and take on leadership roles within the club as they gain experience.
  • Members are expected to communicate effectively with one another and provide feedback and suggestions for improvement.
  • Members are expected to prioritize safety in all grilling activities, and to follow all safety guidelines and protocols established

Club Training

  1. Introduction to grilling: The club provides an introduction to the basics of grilling, including lighting and controlling the grill's temperature.
  2. Meat selection and preparation: Members learn how to select and prepare different types of meat, including marinating and seasoning.
  3. Grill safety: The club emphasizes safety practices to prevent accidents while grilling, including proper grill maintenance and safe handling of equipment.
  4. Advanced grilling techniques: Members have the opportunity to learn advanced techniques such as smoking, indirect grilling, and rotisserie cooking
  5. Sauce and marinade making: Members can learn how to make their own sauces and marinades, including experimenting with different flavor profiles.
  6. Grill maintenance: The club teaches members how to properly clean and maintain their grills to ensure longevity and optimal performance.
  7. Grilling for large groups: Members can learn tips and techniques for cooking for large groups, including timing and preparing food in advance.
  8. Menu planning: Members can learn how to plan menus and pair different foods and drinks together.
  9. Plating and presentation: The club teaches members how to plate and present their dishes to make them look as good as they taste.
  10. Competitions and feedback: Members can participate in grilling competitions and receive feedback on their techniques and dishes, allowing for further development and improvement.
